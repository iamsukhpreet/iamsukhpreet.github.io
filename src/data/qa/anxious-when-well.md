### Answer from Sukhpreet Kaur:

Yes, it is absolutely normal and actually quite common. This is often what we call "free-floating anxiety." 

When your life is going well, your brain might sometimes "search" for a problem to solve because it's so used to being in a state of high alert or survival mode. This can be due to:

1.  **Habituated Stress**: Your nervous system is tuned to a high level of stress, so "calm" feels unfamiliar and even "dangerous" to your brain.
2.  **Anticipatory Anxiety**: The feeling that "the other shoe is about to drop."
3.  **Generalized Anxiety (GAD)**: Where worry is a constant background noise regardless of external circumstances.

**What you can do:**
Instead of asking "Why am I anxious?", try acknowledging it: "My body feels anxious right now, even though I am safe." Grounding exercises (like the ones on our [Panic Relief](/panic-relief) page) can help signal to your brain that it's okay to relax.

If this feeling persists and makes it hard to enjoy your life, talking to a counselor can help you retrain your nervous system to accept and enjoy the "calm."
